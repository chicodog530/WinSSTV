WinSSTV Initial User Instructions

This document will help you get started utilizing WinSSTV natively on your machine!

## 1. Setup Your Rig
WinSSTV utilizes the standard Windows WASAPI sound system.

1. Navigate to the **Settings** tab.
2. In the "Audio Devices" section, use the dropdown menus.
3. Select your "Audio Input" (this should point to your Radio's Line-In, Virtual Audio Cable, or Desktop microphone).
4. Select your "Audio Output" (this should point to your Radio's Line-Out or your Desktop speakers to hear your transmission).
5. Ensure the "Sample Rate Input" is locked to 48000 Hz, as this is required for the internal filters.

## 2. Receiving a Signal
Once your input is set up, the application is ALWAYS listening.

1. Navigate to the **Receive** tab. 
2. Ensure there is audio flowing into your selected input.
3. The Waterfall display will immediately show incoming audio frequencies.
4. If you have "Auto" selected in the Mode dropdown, the system will wait for an incoming VIS header (the specific 5-second digital burst at the front of an SSTV image) and lock on automatically.
5. If you *know* the sender is broadcasting in a specific mode without a header (or it's too noisy), select the Mode (like "Martin 1") manually. The application will "eagerly snap" onto the mathematical rhythm of the image and decode instantly!

## 3. Deep-Decoding a WAV File
If you missed an image live but recorded a clean `.wav` audio file (Must be mono, 12kHz or 48kHz), you can decode it faster than real-time.

1. Navigate to the **Receive** tab.
2. Click **Deep Decode...**
3. Select your `.wav` file.
4. WinSSTV will blitz through the file and print the image instantly based on its internal auto-detect or your selected preferred dropdown Mode.

## 4. Transmitting an Image
1. Navigate to the **Transmit** tab.
2. Click the `Open Image` button above the canvas and pick a .jpg or .png to send.
3. Under the image preview, pick your Mode (Wait for the resize logic to kick in against the image bounds).
4. Fill in your Callsign or Text, and click the canvas to stamp text onto the image natively.
5. Press the `Start Playback` button. The application will begin broadcasting precision 48kHz tones to your selected Audio Output!
